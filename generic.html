I am Arezo, a control software engineer with two master's degrees in different fields of engineering and a passion for innovation and problem-solving. I have a strong background and skills in mechatronics, robotics, smart grids, and control algorithms (model predictive,..), as well as electrical engineering. I am currently a research assistant and a graduate teaching assistant at Carleton University, where I apply my expertise to various domains such as autonomous systems and AI. As a research assistant, I design control algorithms to enhance the performance of autonomous systems. I have experience in working with embedded systems and microcontrollers, such as ARM STM32F7 and STM32F4. I have contributed to several projects that involve modeling, simulation, optimization, and testing of complex systems. As a graduate teaching assistant, I lead and supervise more than 100 students for their final design projects, providing them with guidance and feedback. I have received multiple honors and certifications for my academic and professional achievements, and I am always eager to learn new skills and explore new opportunities. My goal is to leverage my knowledge and experience to create innovative and impactful solutions for real-world problems.
